Zola Fairy

Allow me to introduce you to the sweet and lovely Zola - a beloved and beautiful black cat that I had the privilege of adopting from Silver Spring, MD. It was in September 2022 when I first welcomed this tiny, adorable kitten into my home, and since then, she has grown into a remarkable companion.

Zola exudes an air of calm sophistication, yet she possesses a playful spirit that never fails to bring a smile to my face. Her fur is incredibly soft to the touch, and her solid black coat adds an air of elegance to her overall appearance. One cannot help but be captivated by her gracefully long tail, which was the first thing I noticed upon initially meeting her.

One of the greatest joys in my life is witnessing the special bond that Zola shares with her little sister, Muse. Their playful interactions are a daily source of entertainment and warmth in our home. From playful chase games to synchronized pouncing, their shared adventures create a lively and joyful atmosphere that fills my days with happiness.

My parents' house has two male cats named Cheddar and Colby who were an important part of my life for many years until I moved away, so I wanted to honor them with my own cat's name. I decided on Zola, taken from gorgonzola, to continue the cheese theme established with her predecessors, and her middle name Fairy is to reflect her elegance.

Muse Pixie

I met Muse as a kitten in December 2022 at a Petsmart in Washington, DC, where she and her three siblings were being housed for a shelter. They all had numbers for names and similar appearances, so it was difficult to tell them apart, but Muse stood out with her energetic and mischievous personality, as she climbed on my arms and shoulders and attempted to jump at her own reflection in a window. I was also in awe of her appearance, as I had never had a tri-color tabby before, and her eye-catching collage of colors makes her a suitable opposite to Zola's pure midnight-black.

My adoption application was approved and I happily took her home. It took a couple days for Zola to adjust to her presence, but they soon became the bonded sisters they are now.

Muse kept me on my feet, as her playfulness led her to many silly predicaments. For a while, I referred to her as Muse the Menace. As she got older, she mellowed out, but it's clear she's the same spirited calitabby I fell in love with in DC, while still being cuddly and loving to me and her sister.

For her name, I decided not to continue the cheese-theme, instead opting for a pun, as "Muse" sounds like "mews," which she loves to do a lot. Pixie was chosen to match Zola and highlight her personality.
